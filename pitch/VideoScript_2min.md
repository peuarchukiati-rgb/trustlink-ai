---

Meet Aom. A freelance designer in Ho Chi Minh City — steady income, every bill paid on time, savings
every month. She applies for a small business loan, and the bank rejects her. Why? She has no credit
history. To a traditional credit bureau, Aom is invisible.

Over 1.4 billion adults are locked out of credit the same way — freelancers, gig workers, small
businesses — not because they're risky, but because the system only reads a credit file they don't
have.

So we built TrustLink AI — an explainable Trust Intelligence and Underwriting platform for Shinhan's
Open Banking challenge. Instead of asking "What's your credit history?", it asks "How do you actually
manage money?" — reading Open Banking behaviour and trusted alternative data like pay slips,
insurance, and spending.

Across six trust dimensions, TrustLink gives Aom a Trust Score of 742 out of 1000 — Strong tier. The
same borrower the bureau rejected, TrustLink approves — with a responsible, affordability-checked
offer of 45 million dong. And every number is explainable.

Three things make us different. One: fraud is an independent veto — a high score can never buy back a
fraud signal. Two: we separate the Trust Score from a Confidence Score — a thin file lowers our
certainty, never unfairly the borrower's score. Three: a human always approves. AI recommends; a
person decides.

And because it's for a bank, every data pull runs on scoped, revocable consent from approved
partners, with a reproducible audit trail. In validation, the engine separates risk cleanly and
catches one hundred percent of over-declared fraud — a prototype on synthetic data, but a proven,
explainable mechanism.

TrustLink AI turns the invisible into the approved. Financial inclusion, done responsibly.
